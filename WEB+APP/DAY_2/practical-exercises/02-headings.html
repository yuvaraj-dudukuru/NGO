Heading Level 1

<h1> is the most important heading on the page. There is usually only one per page — it names the main topic, like a book title.

Heading Level 2

<h2> marks a major section under the h1, like a chapter within the book.

Heading Level 3

<h3> is a sub-section inside an h2 — a topic within a chapter.

Heading Level 4

<h4> is a smaller sub-point inside an h3.

Heading Level 5

<h5> is a minor heading, used for finer detail inside an h4.

Heading Level 6

<h6> is the lowest, least important heading level — for the smallest sub-points.
