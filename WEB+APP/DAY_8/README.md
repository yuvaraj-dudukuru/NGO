## 🎯 The Planning Process (used in every plan)

1. **Problem Statement** — what the app solves, for whom, and why it matters.
2. **Features** — a prioritized list (must-have = MVP, nice-to-have = later).
3. **Screens** — the screens the app needs.
4. **User Flow** — how users move through screens to accomplish goals.
5. **Wireframes** — low-detail sketches of each screen's layout.
6. **Navigation Structure** — how all the screens connect.

> Planning before coding is one of the most valuable professional habits. These blueprints mean
> that when Flutter coding begins on Day 9, there's a clear design to build from — no staring at
> a blank screen.

## 🔑 Key Takeaways from Day 8

- A **mobile app** runs natively on a device and can use its hardware; it differs from a web app
  in installation, device access, and offline performance.
- The **mobile ecosystem** has four layers: hardware → OS → applications → app stores.
- Apps come in four types: **native, web, hybrid, cross-platform** — each with trade-offs.
- **Cross-platform** development (e.g. **Flutter**) lets one codebase run on both iOS and Android,
  saving time and cost — which is why businesses prefer it.
- Good apps separate concerns into **presentation, business logic, and data** layers, and balance
  great **UI** (looks) with great **UX** (experience).
- **Professional development always starts with planning.**
