## 1. Executive Summary

This report analyzes RetailMart's recent sales (8 orders across 3 cities and 3 categories).
After cleaning several data-quality issues, the analysis shows that **Electronics drives the
most revenue** despite selling in low quantities, **Pune is the strongest market**, and
**Grocery generates volume but little revenue**. One serious data-entry error (an order
recorded as ₹999,999) was detected and corrected. We recommend **prioritizing Electronics
inventory, investing in the Pune market, and adding data-entry validation.**

## 2. Business Problem

RetailMart's management asked: *Which cities and categories drive revenue, who are our top
customers, and is there anything unusual in the data?* The goal is to guide inventory and
marketing decisions.

## 3. Dataset Description

- **Source:** `retailmart_sales.csv`
- **Period:** 1–5 May 2026
- **Size:** 8 rows × 7 columns (**7 unique orders** after removing a duplicate)
- **Columns:** OrderID, Customer, City, Category, Amount, Quantity, OrderDate

## 4. Data Cleaning Summary

- Removed **1 duplicate** order (OrderID 1003).
- Converted **OrderDate** from text to a date type.
- Standardized **customer and city** text (case and spacing).
- Corrected an **invalid negative quantity** (-2 → median).
- Detected and corrected an **outlier amount** (999,999 → median); filled 1 **missing amount**
  with the median.

**Result:** a clean, validated dataset with no missing values or duplicates.

## 5. Key Findings

1. **Electronics** is the top revenue category, driven by a few high-value orders.
2. **Pune** has the most orders and the highest revenue — the strongest market.
3. **Grocery** sells in high quantities but contributes little revenue.
4. Buying patterns differ by category: Electronics = low quantity/high value;
   Grocery = high quantity/low value.
5. A data-quality issue (₹999,999 entry) revealed **weak input controls**.

## 6. Visualizations

> *(Generated by [Phase 4](../assessments/phase4_visualization/); PNG files live in that folder.)*

- **Revenue by Category** (bar): Electronics leads.
- **Revenue by City** (bar): Pune leads.
- **Order Amount distribution** (histogram): most orders modest; revenue depends on a few large
  ones.
- **Quantity vs Amount** (scatter): two distinct buying patterns by category.

## 7. Recommendations

1. **Prioritize Electronics inventory** and prevent stockouts — it drives revenue.
2. **Invest in the Pune market** with targeted marketing.
3. **Use Grocery to attract customers**, then upsell higher-value Electronics.
4. **Add data-entry validation** to prevent errors like the ₹999,999 order.

## 8. Conclusion

After cleaning, the data clearly shows that revenue is concentrated in **high-value Electronics**
and in the **Pune market**, while Grocery drives volume. Acting on these recommendations should
improve both revenue and data quality.
