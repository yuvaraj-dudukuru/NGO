# Insights & Recommendations — GreenCart Capstone

A standalone executive summary of the capstone analysis. (Full report:

---

## The question

GreenCart's revenue is rising, but is profit keeping pace — and if not, why, and where should the
business act?

## The headline

**Revenue grew strongly across FY2025, but profit margin fell from ~27% (Q1) to ~19% (Q4).**
Growth is being bought with discounts. Profit grew, but far more slowly than revenue.

---

## Insights

1. **Margin erosion is real and steady.** Revenue rose ~44% from H1 to H2 while margin slid ~8
   percentage points over the year. The two trends moving in opposite directions *is* the problem.

2. **Discount creep is the driver.** The average order discount roughly doubled across the year
   (~8% → ~18%). Plotted together, margin falls almost exactly as discount rises.

3. **Damage concentrates in thin-margin Electronics.** Electronics is the largest category by
   revenue but the lowest-margin of the majors, so rising discounts hurt the bottom line most there.

4. **The South region is the worst offender.** South generates the most revenue yet earns the
   *lowest* margin of any region — its growth is the most discount-dependent.

5. **Premium is the bright spot.** Under a fifth of customers generate the largest share of revenue
   at the highest margin — the most valuable and most defensible part of the customer base.

---

## Recommendations

| # | Recommendation | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Introduce discount governance** — approval thresholds on discounts, focused first on Electronics and the South region. Halting the discount creep alone protects several margin points. | 🔴 Priority |
| 2 | **Protect and grow Premium** — direct retention/acquisition spend to the high-value, high-margin Premium segment; build a path to migrate Regular customers upward. | 🟢 Invest |
| 3 | **Re-price or rationalise low-margin lines** — review Grocery (lowest margin) and thin Electronics SKUs for pricing, bundling, or assortment changes. | 🟠 Review |
| 4 | **Fix the South region's commercial model** — diagnose its reliance on discounting and rebuild the offer around value rather than price. | 🔴 Priority |
| 5 | **Monitor margin as a first-class KPI** — track profit margin and average discount monthly on the dashboard so erosion is caught early. | 🟢 Sustain |

---

## Expected impact

Restoring discipline on discounting — especially in Electronics and the South — while protecting the
Premium franchise should **re-couple profit growth to revenue growth**, lifting margin back toward the
mid-20s% without sacrificing the top-line momentum the business has built.
