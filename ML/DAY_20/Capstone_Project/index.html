Stage 1 · Define the business problem

The business problem

GreenCart, an online retailer, has seen revenue climb steadily through FY2025 — but leadership is concerned that profit is not keeping pace. The board needs to know:

  • Is profit margin actually eroding, and if so, by how much and where?
  • Which categories, regions, and customer segments drive the gap between revenue and profit?
  • Where should the business focus to restore profitability without sacrificing growth?

The decision this supports: where to tighten discounting and where to invest over the next year.

Stage 3 · Clean the data

Cleaning & validation

The analysis-ready dataset (sales_clean.csv) was produced by applying a standard cleaning and validation discipline:

  • Typing & parsing — dates parsed to ISO, numerics coerced, categories normalised.
  • Derived fields — revenue = unit_price × units × (1 − discount), profit = revenue − cost, margin_pct = profit ÷ revenue.
  • Validation rules — discounts constrained to 0–55%; revenue, cost and units non-negative; every order maps to a known customer; no duplicate order IDs.
  • New vs returning — each order flagged is_new_customer on first appearance.

The output is a single, trustworthy, tidy table — the foundation for everything that follows.

Stage 7 · Generate insights

Insights

1 · Margin erosion is real and steady. Revenue grew ~44% from H1 to H2, yet profit margin fell from ~27% to ~19% across the year. Growth is being bought with discounts.
2 · Discount creep is the driver. The average order discount roughly doubled over the year (~8% → ~18%). Margin tracks it down almost perfectly.
3 · The damage concentrates in thin-margin Electronics. Electronics is the largest category by revenue but the lowest-margin of the majors, so rising discounts hurt it most.
4 · The South region is the worst offender. South leads on revenue but trails badly on margin — its growth is the most discount-dependent.
5 · Premium is the bright spot. Under a fifth of customers generate the largest share of revenue at the highest margin — the most valuable, most defensible part of the base.
Stage 8 · Create recommendations

Recommendations

1
Introduce discount governance priority Set approval thresholds on discounts, focused first on Electronics and the South region. Even halting the discount creep would protect several points of margin.
2
Protect and grow Premium invest Direct retention and acquisition spend toward the high-value, high-margin Premium segment, and design initiatives to migrate Regular customers upward.
3
Re-price or rationalise structurally low-margin lines review Review Grocery (lowest margin) and thin Electronics SKUs for pricing, bundling, or assortment changes.
4
Fix the South region's commercial model priority Investigate why South relies on discounting and rebuild its offer around value rather than price.
5
Monitor margin as a first-class KPI sustain Track profit margin and average discount on the dashboard monthly, so erosion is caught early next time.
