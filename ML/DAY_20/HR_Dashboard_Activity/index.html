2
Define the objectives

Objective, users, and dashboard type

DecisionChoice
Business objectiveMonitor workforce health and get ahead of rising attrition — retain talent and keep teams staffed.
Intended usersHR / People leadership and department heads (strategic + tactical audience).
Dashboard typeA strategic-to-analytical HR dashboard: headline KPIs for leadership, with breakdowns that let HR business partners diagnose the problem.

These decisions guide every later choice — which KPIs lead, which breakdowns matter, and how the story flows.

3
Create a KPI framework

The KPI framework

A small set of headline KPIs (the discipline of restraint), each chosen for relevance, measurability, and a clear benchmark.

6
Add filters

Filters & slicers

Slicers for the dimensions a user needs to focus on — designed for usability (clear labels, sensible defaults, a reset):

  • Department — isolate a team (e.g. Engineering)
  • Location — compare sites / remote vs HQ
  • Tenure band — focus on early-tenure risk
  • Quarter — the time filter (period)
  • Reset — return to the full-year, all-segments view
7
Test usability

Usability checklist

Reviewed against the design checklist before sign-off:

  • Accuracy — KPI figures reconcile to the source dataset; attrition computed consistently (exits ÷ at-risk).
  • Interactivity — every slicer updates all visuals instantly; reset restores the default view.
  • Readability — clean grid, generous spacing, labelled axes, conventional colour, no clutter.
  • Relevance — every KPI maps to the objective; no vanity metrics.
  • Hierarchy — the eye lands on KPIs first, then the trend, then the breakdowns.
  • Performance — renders instantly from a local data file; works offline.

9
Create an executive summary

Executive summary

Situation. Company-wide attrition reached roughly —% in FY2025 and has been rising through the year, with the workforce shrinking on a net basis as exits outpaced hires.

Diagnosis. The losses are not evenly spread. Engineering leads attrition by a wide margin and simultaneously reports the lowest engagement. Across all teams, early-tenure employees (under 18 months) leave at the highest rate, and exit reasons cluster around career growth and compensation.

Recommendations

1
Launch an Engineering retention plan priority Targeted engagement, manager coaching, and a compensation review for the highest-attrition, lowest-engagement team.
2
Fix early-tenure onboarding act now Strengthen the first-18-months experience — onboarding, mentoring, and clear growth paths — where risk is highest.
3
Address the stated exit reasons review Benchmark compensation and build visible career-growth ladders, the two leading reasons people give for leaving.
4
Monitor engagement as an early-warning KPI sustain Track engagement monthly by team so attrition risk is caught before it shows up as exits.
