# Executive Summary — Northwind Workforce Analytics (FY2025)

## Situation

Northwind's workforce health deteriorated over FY2025. **Company-wide attrition reached ~25%**, and it
**rose steadily through the year** — monthly exits roughly doubled from Q1 to Q4. With **164 exits against
101 new hires**, the company was **net-shrinking** by year-end (≈497 active employees).

## Diagnosis — the losses are concentrated, not company-wide

1. **Engineering is the hotspot.** Engineering's attrition rate (~36%) is well above every other
   department and roughly double the company average. It is, on its own, the largest driver of total losses.

2. **Engagement explains it.** Engineering also reports the **lowest engagement score** (~3.0 vs ~3.5–3.8
   elsewhere). Low engagement is a leading indicator of attrition, and the two line up precisely.

3. **Risk is front-loaded by tenure.** **Early-tenure employees (under 18 months) leave at the highest
   rate (~37%)**, with attrition falling as tenure grows. This points to an onboarding and
   early-experience problem, not a broad one.

4. **People leave for growth and pay.** Exit reasons cluster around **career growth** and **compensation**,
   especially in Engineering.

## Recommendations

| # | Recommendation | Priority | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Engineering retention plan** — targeted engagement, manager coaching, compensation review | 🔴 Priority | Highest attrition + lowest engagement = biggest, most addressable loss |
| 2 | **Fix early-tenure onboarding** — structured onboarding, mentoring, clear growth paths in the first 18 months | 🟠 Act now | Where attrition risk is highest across all teams |
| 3 | **Address stated exit reasons** — benchmark compensation; build visible career ladders | 🟠 Review | Directly targets the two leading reasons people give |
| 4 | **Monitor engagement as an early-warning KPI** — track monthly by team | 🟢 Sustain | Catches risk before it becomes exits |

## Expected impact

Concentrating effort on **Engineering** and on the **first 18 months** of tenure targets the two places
where attrition is most severe and most preventable. Lifting engagement in the at-risk team and
strengthening early-tenure retention should **slow the rise in attrition and return the company to net
headcount growth**.
